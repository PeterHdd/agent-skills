## Raw Input

Support ticket volume for enterprise integration issues rose 65% QoQ. Competitor Acme launched a native Salesforce connector in August. Our Salesforce integration is still in beta. Engineering estimates 8 weeks to GA. Enterprise churn exit surveys cite "integration reliability" (72%) and "slow support response" (41%).

## Generated Executive Summary

Enterprise account churn accelerated in Q3, erasing $14.6M in net ARR despite strong SMB growth. Integration reliability -- amplified by a competitor's GA-ready Salesforce connector -- is the primary driver. Without intervention, Q4 enterprise renewal pipeline ($22M) is at elevated risk.

## 2. KEY FINDINGS
- **Enterprise churn tripled QoQ**: 340 lost accounts ($18.2M ARR) versus 110 in Q2, **signaling a systemic retention crisis, not seasonal variance**.
- **Integration failures are the root cause**: 72% of exit surveys cite integration reliability; support tickets for integration issues rose 65% QoQ. **The Salesforce beta is the single largest friction point.**
- **Competitor timing compounded losses**: Acme's native Salesforce connector launched Aug 12; our enterprise churn spiked 40% in the 6 weeks following. **Market window is narrowing.**
- **NPS erosion signals future churn**: Enterprise NPS fell 14 points (52 to 38), **predicting an additional 8-12% churn in Q4 if unaddressed** (based on historical NPS-to-churn correlation).

## 3. BUSINESS IMPACT
At current trajectory, Q4 enterprise churn could reach $9-11M ARR. Accelerating Salesforce GA and stabilizing integration support could recover 60-70% of at-risk renewals (~$13-15M pipeline protection). Total cost of remediation is estimated at $400K (engineering sprint + dedicated support pod).
